We study **human-machine collaborative perception in complex environments** such as autonomous driving, aviation, and intelligent cockpits.

### Key Topics

- **Driver risk perception** — modeling how drivers perceive and respond to risks in manual vs. automated driving, under varied conditions
- **Cognitive load in intelligent cockpits** — understanding the interplay between driver state, automation level, and situational awareness
- **Multimodal physiological sensing** — fusing eye-tracking, electrodermal activity (EDA), and other signals to develop novel human-machine co-perception and intervention techniques
- **System resilience** — enhancing overall human-machine system robustness and operational efficiency

### Representative Work

- *Visionary Co-Driver* — enhancing driver perception of potential risks with LLM and HUD (IEEE T-ITS, 2025)
- *Differed risk perception in manual and automated driving* (IJHCI, 2024)
- *Dangerous Slime* — a game for improving situation awareness in automated driving (AutoUI, 2023)
